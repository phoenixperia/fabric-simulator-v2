# Renderer Principles
# FIERO Fabric Simulator V2

---

# Core Principle

V2 must remain:

```txt
a structured luxury tailoring renderer
```

NOT:

```txt
an unstable AI image experiment
```

---

# 01 — Structural Realism First

Readable garment structure is prioritized over visual spectacle.

---

# 02 — Silhouette Stability

Stable silhouettes must remain preserved across all garments.

---

# 03 — Fixed Body Architecture

The mannequin remains fixed.

Garments adapt to the mannequin.

---

# 04 — Readable Layering

All garment layers must remain independently readable.

---

# 05 — Controlled Expansion

OUTER systems expand from MID structure.

NOT from new body generation.

---

# 06 — Projection Follows Geometry

Fabric projection must reinforce garment structure.

---

# 07 — Soft Luxury Realism

Soft realism is prioritized over harsh cinematic contrast.

---

# 08 — Subtle Outlines

Outlines support readability without becoming visible illustration lines.

---

# 09 — Validation Before Production

Unstable systems must never enter production.

---

# 10 — Stability Before Expansion

Stable foundations must exist before adding new systems.

---

# 11 — Reuse Before Rebuild

New systems should inherit existing architecture whenever possible.

---

# 12 — AI Supports Structure

AI assists renderer workflows.

AI does not replace renderer architecture.

---

# 13 — Customer Trust Matters

Customer-facing rendering must feel believable and premium.

---

# 14 — Discipline Enables Scalability

Scalability depends on disciplined architecture.

---

# 15 — Elegant Restraint

Subtle realism is more luxurious than exaggerated detail.

---

# 16 — Renderer Identity Preservation

The renderer must not drift into:

- fashion illustration
- cinematic AI artwork
- unstable experimentation
- exaggerated fashion abstraction
